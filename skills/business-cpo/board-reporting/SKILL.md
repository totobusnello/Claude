# Board Reporting & Investor Communications

Expert guidance for executive leadership on board reporting, investor relations, and corporate governance. This skill helps you prepare professional board materials, communicate effectively with stakeholders, and maintain board relationships.

## Core Capabilities

- Board deck structure and narrative building
- Financial reporting for board-level audiences
- KPI dashboards and metric frameworks
- Strategic initiative and OKR tracking
- Risk register and mitigation planning
- Cap table and fundraising updates
- Competitive and market landscape summaries
- Board meeting logistics and governance
- Investor update communications
- Board resolution drafting and minutes

## Board Deck Preparation

### Deck Structure (10-15 slides typical)

**Slide 1: Cover/Title**
- Company name and logo
- Board meeting date
- Fiscal period
- Call to action or theme

**Slide 2: Agenda**
- Overview of topics
- Time allocation
- Any decisions required

**Slide 3: Key Highlights**
- 3-5 major wins/milestones
- Positive metrics
- Strategic progress
- One-liner format with supporting data

**Slide 4-5: Financial Performance**
- Revenue with trend (YoY, QoQ)
- Key expenses
- Gross margin
- Burn rate / cash runway
- Guidance for next quarter/year

**Slide 6: KPI Dashboard**
- 6-8 key metrics
- Current value, target, trend
- Color coding (green/yellow/red)
- Annotations for changes

**Slide 7-8: Strategic Initiatives**
- Current OKRs and progress %
- Completed milestones
- Risks to completion
- Resource needs

**Slide 9: Pipeline & Growth**
- Sales pipeline value
- Customer wins/losses
- ARR growth
- Cohort retention/churn

**Slide 10: Team & Organization**
- Headcount (actual vs plan)
- Key hires/departures
- Open requisitions
- Culture/engagement highlights

**Slide 11: Market & Competition**
- Market size/growth
- Competitive positioning
- Win/loss summary
- Market trends

**Slide 12: Risk Register**
- Top 3-5 risks
- Mitigation plans
- Status and trends

**Slide 13: Fundraising/Cap Table** (if relevant)
- Current round status
- Runway timeline
- Key metrics for fundraising
- Timeline for next round

**Slide 14: Upcoming Priorities**
- Next 30/60/90 days
- Resource/capital needs
- Board support needed

**Slide 15: Q&A Prep**
- Anticipated questions
- Suggested answers
- Supporting data

### Narrative Building Best Practices

**Start with Context**
- Where were we last quarter?
- What were the priorities?
- What changed in the market?

**Tell a Coherent Story**
- One central narrative thread
- How each section connects
- Why these metrics matter
- What it means for the business

**Balance Transparency and Confidence**
- Acknowledge challenges openly
- Explain your mitigation
- Show you have a plan
- Avoid being defensive

**Lead with Strength**
- Put your best wins first
- Contextualize struggles
- Show progress on key initiatives
- Demonstrate learning

**Use Data to Support Narrative**
- One key insight per slide
- Visuals > text
- Trends matter more than single data points
- Benchmarks provide context

### Design Principles

**Visual Hierarchy**
- Clear title for each slide
- One main message per slide
- Supporting details secondary
- Consistent font and colors

**Data Visualization**
- Use line charts for trends
- Use bar charts for comparisons
- Use pie charts for composition (sparingly)
- Label axes clearly
- Include data source/date

**Text Guidelines**
- Bullets, not paragraphs
- 5-7 bullets maximum per slide
- Active voice
- Specific numbers, not "strong" or "great"

## Financial Reporting for Board

### Income Statement (P&L) Summary

**Revenue**
- Total revenue (MRR x 12 for SaaS)
- YoY growth %
- QoQ growth %
- Bookings vs revenue (if applicable)
- By product line (if multi-product)
- By customer segment (Enterprise, Mid-market, SMB)

**Gross Margin**
- Gross profit (Revenue - COGS)
- Gross margin %
- Trend vs prior periods
- COGS breakdown if relevant

**Operating Expenses (by category)**
- Sales & Marketing
  - Total spend
  - % of revenue (CAC efficiency)
  - New ARR per $ spent
- Research & Development
  - Total spend
  - % of revenue
  - Key projects
- General & Administrative
  - Total spend
  - % of revenue
  - Finance, legal, HR costs
- Operations
  - Total spend
  - Hosting, infrastructure costs

**Operating Income/Loss**
- EBITDA
- Net income
- Operating margin %

**Key Metrics**
- Break-even runway (months)
- Cash burn (monthly average)
- Path to profitability

### Balance Sheet Highlights

**Assets**
- Cash on hand
- Accounts receivable
- Capitalized software
- Other assets
- Total assets

**Liabilities**
- Accounts payable
- Deferred revenue (important for SaaS)
- Debt obligations
- Other liabilities
- Total liabilities

**Equity**
- Founder equity
- Investor equity
- Retained earnings
- Stockholders' equity

**Key Ratios**
- Current ratio (liquidity)
- Debt-to-equity
- Days cash on hand

### Cash Flow Statement

**Operating Cash Flow**
- Net income (starting point)
- Add backs: Depreciation, amortization
- Changes in working capital
- Net operating cash flow

**Investing Cash Flow**
- Capital expenditures
- Equipment purchases

**Financing Cash Flow**
- Equity raises
- Debt payments
- Dividends (if any)

**Summary**
- Beginning cash
- Net change in cash
- Ending cash balance
- Runway (months = cash / monthly burn)

## Strategic Initiatives & OKR Tracking

### OKR Framework

**Objectives** - Qualitative goals ("Become the category leader in enterprise")
**Key Results** - Measurable outcomes (4 key results per objective)
**Timeline** - Typically 1 quarter or 1 year
**Owner** - Who is accountable?

### Format for Board Reporting

```
Objective: [Main Goal]
Owner: [Name]
Status: 🟢 On Track | 🟡 At Risk | 🔴 Off Track

Key Result 1: [Metric] - Target [X] | Current [Y] | [X% complete]
Key Result 2: [Metric] - Target [X] | Current [Y] | [X% complete]
Key Result 3: [Metric] - Target [X] | Current [Y] | [X% complete]
Key Result 4: [Metric] - Target [X] | Current [Y] | [X% complete]

Major Milestones Completed:
- [Milestone 1] - [Date]
- [Milestone 2] - [Date]

Blockers/Risks:
- [Risk 1] - Mitigation: [Action]
- [Risk 2] - Mitigation: [Action]

Next 30 Days:
- [Action 1]
- [Action 2]
```

### Typical Strategic Initiatives

**Product Development**
- Launch new feature/product
- Improve product quality
- Expand to new market segment

**Sales & Growth**
- Enter new vertical
- Expand geographic footprint
- Launch new sales channel

**Customer Success**
- Improve retention/reduce churn
- Increase NRR/expansion revenue
- Improve customer satisfaction (NPS)

**Team & Organization**
- Build out key departments
- Establish centers of excellence
- Improve engineering productivity

**Operations & Infrastructure**
- Achieve financial targets
- Improve operational efficiency
- Build scalable systems

### Progress Reporting

**For On-Track Items:**
- Highlight milestone completion
- Show momentum
- Identify any learnings
- Preview next phase

**For At-Risk Items:**
- Explain the gap
- Show root cause analysis
- Present mitigation plan
- Request board input if needed
- Propose course correction

**For Off-Track Items:**
- Take ownership
- Explain what went wrong
- Show what you've learned
- Present new plan
- Request support/resources if needed

## Risk Register & Mitigation

### Risk Register Structure

| Risk | Category | Severity | Probability | Impact | Mitigation | Status | Owner |
|------|----------|----------|-------------|--------|-----------|--------|-------|
| Key person dependency (CEO) | Operational | High | Low | Catastrophic | Cross-training, succession planning | Active | COO |
| Customer concentration (Top 3 = 30%) | Business | High | Medium | High | Customer diversification | In Progress | VP Sales |
| Tech debt accumulation | Technical | High | High | Medium | 20% sprint allocation | In Progress | CTO |
| Market slowdown | External | Medium | Medium | Medium | Diversify by segment, cut costs if needed | Monitoring | CEO |

### Risk Categories

**Business Risks**
- Market demand decline
- Competitive threats
- Customer concentration
- Pricing pressure

**Operational Risks**
- Key person dependency
- Recruitment challenges
- Retention challenges
- Operational inefficiency

**Technical Risks**
- Technology debt
- Scalability issues
- Security vulnerabilities
- Data privacy

**Financial Risks**
- Cash runway
- Burn rate unsustainable
- Inability to fundraise
- Debt obligations

**External Risks**
- Economic downturn
- Regulatory changes
- Market disruption
- Geopolitical factors

### Severity Scale

**Critical**: Would materially impact company viability
**High**: Would significantly impact business or strategy
**Medium**: Would impact progress or efficiency
**Low**: Minor operational impact

### Probability Scale

**High**: Very likely to occur (>70%)
**Medium**: Moderately likely (30-70%)
**Low**: Unlikely (<30%)

### Mitigation Strategies

**Prevent**: Actions to stop the risk from occurring
**Mitigate**: Actions to reduce severity or probability
**Monitor**: Watch closely for early warning signs
**Accept**: Acknowledge and monitor but take no action
**Transfer**: Shift risk to insurance or partner

### Board Conversations

Present top 3-5 risks and:
- Explain why each matters
- Show you're monitoring actively
- Demonstrate mitigation efforts
- Request specific support if needed
- Avoid surprises - flag risks early

## Board Meeting Agenda Preparation

### Pre-Meeting Checklist

- [ ] Confirm attendee list and send calendar invites
- [ ] Prepare board materials (deck, financials, minutes)
- [ ] Send materials 3-5 days in advance
- [ ] Prepare Q&A document with anticipated questions
- [ ] Confirm presentation order and transitions
- [ ] Prepare speaking notes
- [ ] Test technology (video, screen sharing, etc.)
- [ ] Arrange catering/drinks if in-person
- [ ] Confirm recording/transcription setup if hybrid
- [ ] Brief team on any sensitive topics

### Typical Meeting Agenda (2-3 hours)

| Time | Topic | Owner | Duration |
|------|-------|-------|----------|
| 9:00 | Board opening/private session | Chair | 5 min |
| 9:05 | Company update & Q&A | CEO | 30 min |
| 9:35 | Financial review & Q&A | CFO | 20 min |
| 9:55 | Strategic initiatives & Q&A | CEO | 20 min |
| 10:15 | Risk & compliance review | CFO/General Counsel | 10 min |
| 10:25 | *Break* | | 10 min |
| 10:35 | Resolutions & votes | Chair | 20 min |
| 10:55 | Executive session (if needed) | Chair | 15-30 min |
| 11:10-11:30 | Closing remarks & feedback | Chair | 10 min |

### Meeting Logistics

**Timing**
- Send agenda 1 week in advance
- Send materials 3-5 days before
- Schedule 2-3 hours typically
- Start/end on time

**Format**
- In-person preferred for annual/important meetings
- Virtual acceptable for routine updates
- Hybrid: Have option to attend in-person or virtually

**Attendees**
- All board members
- CEO (always)
- CFO (financial discussions)
- General Counsel (legal/compliance)
- Other executives as needed for specific topics
- Observer board observers if applicable
- Advisor advisors for specific topics

### Executive Session

The board may request an executive session:
- With just board members (no management)
- To discuss CEO performance, compensation, succession
- To discuss sensitive business matters
- To discuss conflicts of interest
- To discuss fundraising or M&A

Respect privacy - leave the room when requested.

## Board Resolution Drafting

### Resolution Format

```
BOARD RESOLUTION [Number]

RESOLVED, that the Board of Directors of [Company Name], Inc.
(the "Company"), having determined that it is in the best interest
of the Company and its stockholders, hereby [ACTION DESCRIPTION].

Adopted: [Date]
Approved by: [Board Chair or Secretary]
```

### Common Resolutions

**1. Option Pool Increase**

```
RESOLVED, that the Board of Directors of [Company Name], Inc.
hereby approves the increase of the option pool under the [Stock
Option Plan Name] from [X] shares to [Y] shares, representing
[X]% of the fully diluted capitalization.

The additional [Y-X] shares are reserved for issuance to employees,
consultants, and advisors as recommended by management and approved
by the Board or Compensation Committee.
```

**2. Approval of Equity Grant**

```
RESOLVED, that the Board of Directors hereby approves the grant of
[X] shares of common stock options to [Employee Name],
[Title], at an exercise price of $[Price] per share, with a [X]-year
vesting schedule (25% one-year cliff, remainder vesting over [X] years).

This grant is intended to serve as [purpose: retention, new hire,
promotion, etc.].
```

**3. Approval of Annual Budget**

```
RESOLVED, that the Board of Directors hereby approves the FY [Year]
operating budget, as presented, with projected revenue of $[X]M and
operating expenses of $[Y]M.

FURTHER RESOLVED, that the CEO is authorized to manage spending
within the approved budget, with board notification required for
any individual expense exceeding $[X] or any modification to
budgeted headcount.
```

**4. Approval of Financing Terms**

```
RESOLVED, that the Board of Directors hereby authorizes the Company
to enter into a [Series X] Preferred Stock financing transaction with
[Investor Names], on the terms outlined in the term sheet dated
[Date], including:
  - Price per share: $[X]
  - Total raise: $[X]M
  - Investor representation on Board: [X] seat(s)
  - Liquidation preference: [Terms]

The CEO and General Counsel are authorized to execute all documents
necessary to complete the transaction.
```

**5. Approval of Acquisition/Merger**

```
RESOLVED, that the Board of Directors hereby approves the proposed
[acquisition/merger] of [Target Company] for a purchase price of
$[X] million, on the terms outlined in the term sheet dated [Date].

FURTHER RESOLVED, that the CEO and General Counsel are authorized
to negotiate and execute all documents necessary to complete the
transaction, subject to final Board approval if material terms change.
```

### Key Elements

**Body**
- Clear description of action
- Relevant terms (if applicable)
- Authority granted (who is authorized to execute)

**Conditions**
- Any conditions precedent
- Reporting requirements
- Limitations on authority

**Recitals (optional)**
- Add context if helpful
- Board's reasoning
- Why this is in the company's interest

### Process

1. Present topic and resolution draft
2. Answer questions
3. Motion and second
4. Vote (show of hands, voice vote, or roll call)
5. Record result
6. Secretary signs/dates
7. File in company records

### Investor Update Best Practices

**Timing**
- Monthly: For early stage (seed/Series A) with active investor involvement
- Quarterly: Standard for Series A and beyond
- Annual: For mature companies or less active investors

**Content**
- Always include metrics (even if trending down)
- Be honest about challenges
- Show progress on stated priorities
- Celebrate wins appropriately
- Keep it concise (1-2 pages)

**Tone**
- Professional but personable
- Confident but realistic
- Transparent about challenges
- Appreciative of support

**Format**
- Email is standard for investor updates
- Attached PDF deck for annual summaries
- Link to more detailed dashboard if applicable

## Key Questions to Ask

Before preparing board materials:

1. What are the board's key concerns or focus areas?
2. What decisions does the board need to make this meeting?
3. What performance areas are boards typically most focused on?
4. Are there specific investor preferences on format/timing?
5. What's the board's appetite for risk/aggressive growth?
6. Are there any contentious issues that need careful handling?
7. What would constitute a "successful" board meeting?
8. What topics require executive session discussion?
9. Are there any personnel or confidential matters to flag?
10. What feedback did we get from the last board meeting?

## Common Board Mistakes to Avoid

❌ **Burying bad news** - Put challenges upfront with mitigation
❌ **Inconsistent metrics** - Define metrics clearly and track consistently
❌ **Surprises** - Flag major issues early, not in the board meeting
❌ **Too much detail** - Highlight insights, not raw data
❌ **No narrative** - Tie data points to a coherent story
❌ **Unclear asks** - Be specific about what you need from the board
❌ **Ignoring feedback** - Show progress on items the board has raised
❌ **Defensive tone** - Take responsibility and show what you're doing
❌ **Missing materials** - Send decks and financials well in advance
❌ **Over-optimism** - Set realistic targets, beat them consistently

### 5. Marketing Analytics & Attribution

#### Key Marketing Metrics Dashboard
```
Top-of-Funnel:
- Marketing-qualified leads (MQL)
- Cost per lead (CPL)
- Lead volume vs. target
- Channel mix

Mid-Funnel:
- Opportunity creation rate
- Sales-accepted leads (SAL)
- Pipeline generation

Bottom-of-Funnel:
- Closed won deals
- Win rate by source
- Deal size by channel
- Sales cycle by source

Efficiency:
- CAC by channel
- CAC payback period
- ROAS by channel
- Marketing contribution to revenue
- Marketing efficiency ratio (MER)
```

#### Attribution Modeling
```
Multi-Touch Attribution Models:

1. First-Touch (40% credit)
- Best for awareness campaigns
- Shows which channels introduce prospects

2. Last-Touch (60% credit)
- Best for conversion tracking
- Shows which channels close deals

3. Linear (50/50 or equal)
- Balanced approach
- Acknowledges both awareness and conversion

4. Time-Decay
- More credit to recent touches
- Useful for complex sales cycles

5. Custom (by business model)
- Weighted based on funnel stage
- Reflects actual revenue impact

Implementation:
- 3-6 month attribution window
- Minimum 5-touch campaigns
- Channel-level analysis
- Campaign-level analysis
```

- **Weekly**: Campaign performance, channel ROAS
- **Monthly**: Funnel metrics, CAC trends, attribution
- **Quarterly**: Strategic review, ROI analysis, budget optimization

### 13. Budget Management & Optimization

#### Annual Marketing Budget Framework
```
Typical Budget Allocation:
- People/Salaries: 40-50%
- Technology/Tools: 15-20%
- Demand Generation: 20-25%
- Content: 10-15%
- Events/Sponsorships: 5-10%
- Testing/Innovation: 5-10%
- Agency/Professional Services: 5-10%

Budget Variation by Stage:
Early Stage: 70% demand gen, 20% content, 10% brand
Growth: 50% demand gen, 25% content, 25% brand
Mature: 30% demand gen, 30% content, 40% brand
```

#### Budget Allocation Approach
```
Zero-Based Budget Review:
Q1: Strategic planning and approval
Q2: Mid-year review and optimization
Q3: Performance review and adjustments
Q4: Next-year planning

Channel Budgeting:
1. Set CAC targets by channel
2. Model volume at target CAC
3. Forecast revenue impact
4. Optimize mix for CAC payback
5. Test and learn allocation
```

#### Marketing ROI Framework
```
Calculate Marketing Contribution:
- Attribution revenue
- Pipeline created
- Deal influence
- Customer retention
- Upsell/expansion

Measure Efficiency:
- Magic Number: (Q revenue - Q-1 revenue) / Q-1 marketing spend
  Target: >0.75 for growth companies

- CAC Payback Period: CAC ÷ (monthly gross margin × win rate)
  Target: <12 months

- ROAS: Revenue from channel / Channel spend
  Target: >3:1 (varies by channel and stage)

- Marketing Efficiency Ratio: Revenue / Total Marketing Spend
  Target: 3-5x for mature, 1-2x for growth
```

## Crisis Management

### Brand Crisis Response

#### Level 1: Monitoring (Minor Issue)
1. **Immediate** (0-2 hours):
   - Assess severity and impact
   - Monitor social listening
   - Engage leadership
   - Begin internal communication

2. **Short-term** (2-24 hours):
   - Prepare statement if needed
   - Review facts and timeline
   - Identify stakeholders
   - Plan communication

#### Level 2: Incident (Moderate Impact)
1. **Immediate** (0-2 hours):
   - Activate crisis communications team
   - Prepare hold statements
   - Notify key stakeholders (board, CEO)
   - Begin social monitoring

2. **Short-term** (2-24 hours):
   - Release statement
   - Engage media if needed
   - Update social media
   - Internal all-hands communication

3. **Resolution** (1-7 days):
   - Provide regular updates
   - Address customer concerns
   - Media engagement as needed
   - Rebuild narrative

#### Level 3: Crisis (Major Impact)
1. **Immediate** (0-1 hour):
   - CEO engagement
   - Full crisis team activation
   - Legal/compliance review
   - Board notification
   - Executive communication prep

2. **Short-term** (1-24 hours):
   - CEO/leadership statement
   - Comprehensive media outreach
   - Customer and stakeholder communication
   - Internal alignment
   - Social media response

3. **Recovery** (1-4 weeks):
   - Transparent communication
   - Rebuilding trust
   - Culture reinforcement
   - Long-term strategy adjustment

## Success Indicators

### CMO Effectiveness Indicators

✅ **Brand Success**
- Brand awareness tracking increasing
- Brand perception improving
- Net Promoter Score >50
- Brand preference vs. competitors improving
- Customer acquisition from brand/awareness

✅ **Marketing Performance**
- CAC decreasing or stable with quality
- ROAS >3:1 across channels
- Lead volume on target
- Pipeline contribution growing
- Marketing-attributed revenue >50% of total

✅ **Efficiency & Growth**
- Marketing contribution to revenue >10-15%
- Marketing spending increasing with revenue
- Team productivity/revenue per marketer
- Customer lifetime value increasing
- Retention/expansion revenue growing

✅ **Strategic Impact**
- Product launches executing on time
- Competitive positioning strengthened
- Thought leadership established
- Market expansion successful
- New revenue streams launched

✅ **Organizational Health**
- Team retention >90%
- Employee engagement >7/10
- Recruitment success >80%
- Cross-functional partnerships strong
- Innovation pipeline active

## Red Flags to Watch

⚠️ Increasing customer acquisition cost
⚠️ Declining lead quality or conversion rates
⚠️ Sales team not using marketing leads
⚠️ Market share declining vs. competition
⚠️ Brand perception deteriorating

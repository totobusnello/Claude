## 1. Lab Phase Context + Capacity

### Why a Research Lab?

nox-mem is built on the conviction that **Quality** is the primary competitive moat — not features, not marketing. The Q/A/P strategic pivot (2026-05-17) formalizes this: the system's hybrid retrieval architecture must remain empirically defensible against any challenger, at any time, with honest published numbers.

The Lab exists to pursue paper-grade improvements that the product roadmap cannot absorb — experiments that may fail, take longer than a sprint, or produce negative results that are nonetheless published.

### Capacity Model

Post-launch capacity splits as follows:

| Bucket | Allocation | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Lab** | **40%** | Experiments, ablations, paper updates, benchmark runs |
| Product maintenance | ~25% | Bug fixes, schema migrations, community issues |
| GTM / nox-supermem | ~20% | Pricing, onboarding, Stripe integrations |
| Community response | ~15% | GitHub issues, PR reviews, Discord |

The 40% Lab allocation is **protected**. Product feature requests do not pre-empt Lab weeks unless a critical regression requires it.

### Quarterly Cadence

Each calendar quarter allocates **4 dedicated Lab weeks** distributed across the quarter (not necessarily contiguous). Q1 2026 spans **July–September 2026**, immediately following the expected GTM launch window (June 2026).

| Lab week | Target window | Primary focus |
|---|---|---|
| Lab W1 | Early Jul 2026 | Concentration/Coverage experiment setup + corpus design |
| Lab W2 | Mid Jul 2026 | Concentration/Coverage runs + neural reranker baseline |
| Lab W3 | Mid Aug 2026 | Scale stress test + multilingual dataset curation + Benchmark P4 run |
| Lab W4 | Early Sep 2026 | Encrypted-DB overhead + cross-system A/B + Q1 review write-up |

### Output Expectations

Each Lab quarter produces:
- **1–2 publishable findings** (audit doc or paper PR): positive, negative, or null results all count
- **Pre-registered specs** before work starts (see [§4 Governance](#4-lab-governance))
- **Quarterly review doc** at end of quarter summarizing findings and Q2 priorities

This plan is cross-linked to the Q/A/P strategic decision documented in `docs/DECISIONS.md` (D47 caminho C) and the research parking lot in `docs/ROADMAP.md §6`.

### Priority 1: Concentration vs Coverage Paradox *(NEW — highest)*

**v2 status: NEW** — discovered 2026-05-24 via Q4 harness validation.

**Ranking rationale:** The mem0 result (0.8569 nDCG@10 on their golden set) vs nox_mem's 0.6380 on LoCoMo reveals a fundamental architectural question that currently has no clear answer: does **concentrating retrieval on fewer, higher-quality chunks** outperform **broad hybrid coverage** for memory systems? This is simultaneously the highest-impact product question and the most scientifically novel finding from Sat 2026-05-24.

#### Motivation

During Q4 harness validation (Sat 2026-05-24), the following contrast emerged:

| System | Metric | Value | Architecture |
|---|---|---|---|
| **nox_mem** | nDCG@10 | **0.6380** | Hybrid BM25 + Gemini 3072d + RRF, 69k chunks |
| **mem0** | nDCG@10 | **~0.8569** (estimated from gold_hits) | Episodic memory, 500-cap per session, concentration |

The ~19pp gap is the largest single signal produced in the Q4 harness run. **Two competing hypotheses** are both scientifically viable:

- **H1 (Coverage wins at scale):** nox_mem's hybrid retrieval is correct; mem0's higher nDCG@10 is an artifact of their 500-chunk cap causing overfitting to a small corpus that matches their benchmark construction. As corpus grows, concentration degrades.
- **H2 (Concentration wins for personal memory):** mem0's cap strategy is genuinely superior for personal memory systems where recency and episode coherence matter more than exhaustive recall. Broad retrieval introduces noise at any scale.

The honest answer is: **we do not know**. This experiment resolves that.

#### Scope

Phase 1 — Controlled corpus construction:
- Create a **size-matched corpus** at 3 scales: 500 chunks, 5000 chunks, 50000 chunks
- Same content (subset of production entity files + conversation logs)
- Run nox_mem at each scale; measure nDCG@10, MRR, R@10
- Run mem0 at each scale if adapter supports variable-cap

Phase 2 — Retrieval style analysis:
- Classify nox_mem retrieved chunks by source type distribution at each corpus scale
- Measure whether broader corpus *hurts* precision due to RRF dilution (null result would be important)
- Ablate Hard Mutex conditional t=2 at each scale (does mutex help more at scale or less?)

Phase 3 — Report:
- If H1: publish as "hybrid retrieval quality scales — concentration is a shortcut that degrades with corpus growth"
- If H2: publish as "concentration at retrieval time as a viable alternative to broad hybrid search for bounded personal corpora"
- If mixed: characterize the crossover point (below N chunks, concentration wins; above N, hybrid wins)

**Evaluation corpus:** LoCoMo n=100 (same as Q4 live validation), ensuring comparability with the 0.6380 baseline.

#### Risk Assessment

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| mem0 adapter limitations prevent fair comparison at all scales | Medium | Use nox_mem in "concentration mode" (top-k=20 hard cap) as proxy |
| 500-chunk corpus too small for meaningful hybrid eval | Medium | Measure FTS5 coverage separately; flag in results |
| Result challenges Q/A/P Quality narrative | Medium | Honest reporting is the mandate (D47 caminho C); negative results for nox_mem are publishable |
| Corpus construction time exceeds 1 Lab week | Low | Use synthetic augmentation from existing entity files |

#### Outputs

|---|---|---|

| Experiment results | `audits/2026-07-XX-concentration-vs-coverage.md` | End Lab W2 |
| Paper update (§6.5 or §4.5) | `paper/paper-tecnico-nox-mem.md` | End Lab W2 + 1 week |

**Time estimate:** 2 weeks (1 week corpus + harness; 1 week runs + analysis + write-up)

**Dependencies:**

### Priority 2: Neural Reranker Exploration *(MODIFIED — high)*

**v2 status: MODIFIED** — retains core scope from v1; updated to include concentration finding as secondary lens.

**Ranking rationale:** Cross-encoder reranking is a well-established technique with documented +3–8% nDCG@10 improvements in the IR literature. It extends the current 3-layer retrieval stack (FTS5 → Gemini semantic → RRF) with a 4th layer. Blocks a future paper §6.3 addition and addresses the competitive gap vs systems that use neural reranking.

**v2 update:** The concentration/coverage finding (P1) adds a new question: does cross-encoder reranking at top-40 candidates effectively simulate "concentration" within a large corpus? If A13-bge-v2-top40 achieves parity with mem0's nDCG@10, this resolves P1's H1 hypothesis simultaneously. Also: Q4 live validation confirmed p50=12ms / p95=43ms for nox_mem **local** search — A15 latency experiments must use the **VPS prod instance** (p50~940ms embed-dominated) as baseline, not the local FTS5-only number.

#### Motivation

The current retrieval pipeline terminates at RRF fusion (k=60). The literature consistently shows cross-encoder rerankers improve precision in the final ranking pass, particularly for queries where semantic similarity and lexical overlap diverge. G-series ablations (G1–G11) have optimized within the current 3-layer architecture; neural reranking represents the most promising next architectural step.

The key constraint is the **Autonomy pillar**: nox-mem's data stays local or on user-controlled infrastructure. This rules out Cohere Rerank (cloud API, data leaves user control). BGE-reranker-v2-m3 (HuggingFace, Apache 2.0) is the primary candidate — runnable locally via Ollama or directly via ONNX.

#### Scope

Experiment series: **A11–G15 ablations** with reranker as Layer 4 (post-RRF, pre-response):

| Experiment | Configuration | Hypothesis |
|---|---|---|
| A11-baseline | Current G10d config, no reranker | Baseline nDCG@10 (expect ~0.62 on entity-eval-v2) |
| A12-bge-v2 | + BGE-reranker-v2-m3 (top-20 candidates → rerank → top-10) | +3–8% nDCG@10 |
| A13-bge-v2-top40 | + BGE-reranker-v2-m3 (top-40 candidates) | Diminishing returns check + concentration proxy (v2) |
| A14-cross-enc-ms | + ms-marco-MiniLM-L6 (lighter, faster) | Speed/quality tradeoff |
| A15-latency | A12 config with p50/p95/p99 latency measured (VPS prod instance) | Latency regression check vs VPS p50~940ms baseline |

Layer 4 integration point: after RRF produces ranked list, pass top-N candidates to cross-encoder, get scores, re-sort. The reranker receives `(query, chunk_text)` pairs — no schema changes required.

**Autonomy constraint:** All model candidates must run locally or on user-controlled infra. GPU resource (Modal/Replicate) is acceptable for Lab experiments but the production integration must have a CPU-viable fallback.

#### Risk Assessment

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Reranker adds >500ms latency (violates product SLA) | Medium | CPU inference profiling in A15; gate production deployment on p95 < 1500ms total on VPS |
| BGE-reranker underperforms published figures on nox-mem eval | Medium | nox-mem eval is entity-flavored (different from MS-MARCO fine-tuning domain); document domain gap |
| Reranker nullifies section_boost/Hard Mutex gains | Low | Ablate with/without mutex in G15; if redundant, Hard Mutex may be removed |
| GPU resource budget for Lab experiments | Low | Modal Serverless ~$0.001/min A10G; 3-week experiment budget < $20 |

|---|---|---|
| Pre-registration spec | `specs/lab-q1-neural-reranker.md` | Before Lab W2 |
| Ablation results | `audits/2026-08-XX-neural-reranker-ablations.md` | End Lab W2 |
| Paper update (if +5%+) | `paper/paper-tecnico-nox-mem.md` §4.4 | End Lab W3 |

**Time estimate:** 3 weeks (1 week setup + integration; 1 week ablation runs; 1 week analysis + write-up)

**Dependencies:**

### Priority 3: Scale to 250k+ Chunks *(MODIFIED — high)*

**v2 status: MODIFIED** — retains core scope from v1; adds encrypted-DB variant now that A2 Tier 3 P0–P4 is complete.

**Ranking rationale:** nox-mem's current production corpus is ~69k chunks. The product's viability for power users depends on maintaining retrieval quality and latency at 3–5× current scale. This experiment reveals bottlenecks before they become production incidents.

**v2 update:** A2 Tier 3 P0–P4 merged Sat 2026-05-24. The scale test now has a natural encrypted variant: does SQLCipher AES-256-GCM add meaningful overhead at 250k chunks? See also P7 (Encrypted-DB Query Overhead) which isolates this variable independently and should run first.

#### Motivation

The current architecture uses SQLite (FTS5 + sqlite-vec) on a single VPS (Hostinger KVM4, 8GB RAM, 4 vCPU). SQLite is known to scale well for read-heavy workloads up to ~1M rows, but the FTS5 index and sqlite-vec vector store have different scaling characteristics. The Gemini embedding API (batch-limited, rate-limited at 1500 RPM for gemini-embedding-001) is the suspected primary bottleneck for ingestion.

A target of **250k chunks** (~3.6× current prod) represents a realistic 18-month growth trajectory for a power user ingesting daily across multiple workspaces.

#### Scope

- Generate or acquire a **synthetic corpus** of 250k chunks with realistic characteristics:
  - Mix of entity files, markdown docs, conversation logs, code snippets
  - PT-BR and EN content (ratio ~30/70 matching expected user distribution)
  - Realistic chunk sizes (200–600 tokens per chunk, consistent with current distribution)
- Ingest corpus into isolated eval DB (never touch main DB — lesson from incident 2026-05-19)
- Measure at 50k / 100k / 150k / 200k / 250k checkpoints:
  - Ingestion throughput (chunks/min, embed API calls/min, failures)
  - Search latency (p50/p95/p99 for hybrid BM25+semantic+RRF query)
  - Memory footprint (RAM during search, VPS limits)
  - FTS5 index size and query plan (EXPLAIN QUERY PLAN)
  - sqlite-vec query time vs vector count
- **v2 addition:** At the 250k checkpoint, run both plaintext and encrypted (SQLCipher AES-256-GCM) variants; measure overhead delta (P7 results inform interpretation)

**Hypothesis:** FTS5 should remain fast at 250k. sqlite-vec query time scales linearly; expected p50 degradation < 200ms. SQLCipher overhead at 250k estimated +3–7% per A2 Tier 3 spike findings — scale test validates that estimate on a realistic corpus.

#### Risk Assessment

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Synthetic corpus lacks realistic diversity | Medium | Use subset of publicly available datasets (Wikipedia, Stack Overflow, CC-News PT-BR) augmented with template generation |
| sqlite-vec scales worse than expected | Medium | If degradation > 300ms at 250k, evaluate chunked HNSW index or batch-vector approach |
| Gemini API quota exhaustion during test | Low | Use dedicated Gemini project key with elevated quota for Lab week; do not use production key |
| VPS OOM at 250k | Low | Monitor `/proc/meminfo` during test; abort at 80% RAM usage; note failure point |
| SQLCipher adds unexpected overhead at scale | Medium | Isolated by P7 before P3 scale run; findings feed P3 interpretation |

| Artifact | Path | ETA |
|---|---|---|
| Pre-registration spec | `specs/lab-q1-scale-stress.md` | Before Lab W3 |
| Stress test results | `audits/2026-08-XX-scale-stress-250k.md` | End Lab W3 |
| Architecture note (if bottleneck found) | `docs/adr/ADR-XXX-scale-ceiling.md` | End Lab W3 |

**Time estimate:** 2 weeks (1 week corpus setup + tooling; 1 week measurement runs + write-up)

**Dependencies:**
- Synthetic corpus generator OR partner with user willing to share anonymized data
- Isolated eval VPS or Lab VM with equivalent specs to production (Hostinger KVM4)

### Priority 5: Multi-lingual Evaluation *(KEEP — medium)*

**v2 status: KEEP** — no changes from v1. De-prioritized 1 slot (was P4) relative to new higher-urgency items.

**Ranking rationale:** nox-mem's primary user base is PT-BR (Toto's audience), but the paper and competitive positioning claim multi-lingual capability via Gemini embedding (which is multi-lingual by design). This claim needs empirical validation. Ranked medium because it does not block the paper or product milestone, but does strengthen the Autonomy pillar's value for international markets.

#### Motivation

Gemini embedding (`gemini-embedding-001`) is trained on multi-lingual corpora and performs competitively on cross-lingual tasks in Google's internal benchmarks. However, nox-mem has not independently validated this claim on its specific query patterns (temporal, entity-centric, adversarial). The internal golden set is EN-only. PT-BR users (the primary target market) have no eval coverage.

Multi-lingual eval also tests whether the FTS5 BM25 layer degrades for non-Latin scripts or heavily diacriticized text (PT-BR accented characters showed `\b` boundary failures in `[[js-regex-unicode-word-boundary-fails]]` — FTS5 may have analogous issues).

#### Scope

Language targets for Q1 (in priority order):
1. **PT-BR** — primary market; ~50 golden queries covering daily conversation, technical terms, finance vocabulary
2. **ES** — Latin American secondary market; ~25 queries
3. **DE** — European market reference; ~25 queries (German compound words stress tokenizer)
4. **FR** — European market secondary; ~25 queries

For each language:
- Curate 25–50 golden queries with known correct answers in the chunk corpus
- Run hybrid search and record nDCG@10, MRR@10
- Compare against EN baseline (same query semantics, translated)
- Profile whether degradation is in FTS5 layer, semantic layer, or RRF fusion

**Native speaker requirement:** Dataset curation for each language must involve a native speaker review pass. Community contributions welcome (see [§5](#5-open-questions--external-collaborators-welcome)).

#### Risk Assessment

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Gemini embedding underperforms on PT-BR vs EN | Medium | Expected; document delta; if > 10% nDCG gap, flag for language-specific embedding evaluation |
| FTS5 tokenizer degrades on accented characters | Medium | FTS5 uses Unicode-aware tokenizer by default; validate with `\p{L}` whitelist sanitize (lesson `[[unicode-aware-sanitize-for-fts5]]`) |
| Native speaker availability for dataset review | High | Community-first: open GitHub issue inviting native speakers; do not publish without review |
| Dataset construction takes > 1 week | Medium | Scope PT-BR only for Q1 if time-constrained; ES/DE/FR move to Q2 |

### Priority 6: Salience Formula Optimization *(MODIFIED — medium)*

**v2 status: MODIFIED** — scope narrowed. Per-method benchmark Phase B (PR #298) absorbs retrieval-style analysis that previously fell under this priority. P6 now strictly covers **formula weight search only** (BO/CMA-ES on the 4 salience weights).

**Ranking rationale:** The G10d Hard Mutex deployment represents the practical ceiling of hand-tuned boost coefficients. The next-generation approach is automated weight search. Ranked medium because the current formula is deployed and working (+0.79% nDCG / +2.65% MRR vs no-mutex).

**v2 scope reduction:** v1 P5 included both (a) weight search via BO/CMA-ES and (b) cross-query-style analysis. The per-method benchmark Phase B (PR #298) handles (b). This priority now covers only (a) — formula weight search.

#### Motivation

The salience formula v2 (deployed 2026-05-21) uses hand-tuned weights:
```
salience = W_IMPORTANCE×importance + W_RECENCY×recency_score + W_PAIN×pain + W_ACCESS×access_score
W_IMPORTANCE=0.55, W_RECENCY=0.15, W_PAIN=0.10, W_ACCESS=0.20
```

The G-series ablations (G7–G11) established that the formula is **neutral in shadow mode on small corpora** and only contributes meaningfully at production scale. Bayesian Optimization (BO) or CMA-ES could find weights that generalize better across query types.

**Key constraint:** Hyperparameter search must be cross-validated against a **held-out eval set** that was not used in any prior ablation.

#### Scope

Phase 1 (Lab W3): held-out set construction
- 50 queries drawn from nox-mem production logs (anonymized, deduplicated, novel)
- Does NOT include any query from LongMemEval n=100 or entity-eval-v2 (strict separation)
- Native speaker review if PT-BR queries included

Phase 2 (Lab W4): automated weight search only
- **BO approach:** Optuna with TPE sampler; 200 trials; objective = held-out nDCG@10
- **CMA-ES approach:** `cma` Python package; 150 generations; same objective
- Search space: W_IMPORTANCE ∈ [0.40, 0.70], W_RECENCY ∈ [0.10, 0.25], W_PAIN ∈ [0.05, 0.20], W_ACCESS ∈ [0.10, 0.30] (constrained to sum = 1.0)
- Cross-validate winning weights on entity-eval-v2 before any deployment consideration

*(Retrieval-style analysis across query methods deferred to PR #298 per-method Phase B — not in scope here.)*

**Decision gate:** If optimized weights improve held-out nDCG@10 by < 1.0%, the current hand-tuned weights are retained. If ≥ 1.0%, weights advance to shadow mode for 7-day baseline before deployment.

#### Risk Assessment

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Overfitting to held-out set | Medium | Cross-validate on entity-eval-v2 before any deployment |
| BO search takes >4 weeks at scale | Low | Cap at 200 trials (~4h on CPU for fast eval; use g9.db-sized corpus) |
| Optimal weights destabilize Hard Mutex behavior | Low | Run G12-style per-category audit on optimized config before advancing |
| Net result: current weights are optimal | Medium | Valid outcome; publish as "weights are robust to optimization" — still a scientific finding |

|---|---|---|

### Priority 7: Encrypted-DB Query Latency Overhead *(NEW — medium)*

**v2 status: NEW** — unblocked by A2 Tier 3 P0–P4 completion (Sat 2026-05-24).

**Ranking rationale:** A2 Tier 3 (SQLCipher AES-256-GCM encryption) is now merged and in-flight for production. The spike estimated +3–7% query latency overhead — but that was a synthetic micro-benchmark, not a real query-path measurement. This experiment produces the honest production number before the feature is widely deployed, and feeds into P3 (scale test encrypted variant).

#### Motivation

A2 Tier 3 gives nox-mem a genuine Autonomy differentiation: encrypted-at-rest SQLite with user-controlled keys. The engineering question is: **what does encryption actually cost at query time on real hybrid search workloads?**

The concern is not just bulk query overhead but specifically:
- FTS5 tokenizer reads: does SQLCipher page decryption add meaningful overhead per FTS5 lookup?
- sqlite-vec vector scan: 3072d float32 vectors read from encrypted pages — decryption per page during ANN search
- Combined hybrid search: FTS5 + vec + RRF fusion total overhead under encryption

This is also a **paper addition**: publishing honest encrypted-DB query overhead demonstrates nox-mem's Autonomy feature does not come at a prohibitive performance cost (or documents the cost transparently if it does).

#### Scope

- Baseline: g9.db (69495 chunks) plaintext hybrid search, p50/p95/p99 measured (n=100 queries)
- Encrypted: same corpus migrated via A2 Tier 3 migration script, same queries, same measurement
- Breakdown by operation: FTS5 query time, vec query time, RRF fusion time (use F10 Phase C telemetry drilldown — LIVE since PR #283)
- Measurement at 3 corpus sizes: ~70k (current prod), ~150k (mid-scale), ~250k (stress — feeds P3)

**Hypothesis:** SQLCipher overhead < 10% at p50 for hybrid search on 70k chunks. Overhead scales sub-linearly with corpus size if page-cache is warm.

#### Risk Assessment

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| SQLCipher overhead > 15% reveals product positioning risk | Medium | Publish honestly; explore SQLCipher page-cache tuning (cache_size pragma) |
| A2 Tier 3 not fully stable for Lab measurement in Q1 | Low | A2 P0–P4 merged Sat 2026-05-24; P3 (per-user key) targets Sun 2026-05-25 |
| F10 telemetry insufficient granularity for per-op breakdown | Low | Fall back to SQLite EXPLAIN QUERY PLAN + manual timing if F10 Phase C telemetry too coarse |

## 4. Lab Governance

### Pre-Registration

**Every Lab item requires a spec before work starts.** The spec lives at `specs/lab-q1-<name>.md` and defines:
- Hypothesis (falsifiable)
- Exact metrics (what counts as success)
- Eval corpus identity (dataset hash + chunk count)
- Decision gate (what threshold triggers advancement vs parking)
- Time budget (hard cap)

Pre-registration prevents post-hoc rationalization of results. A finding without a pre-registered spec is not publishable as a Lab finding.

### Reproducibility

Every Lab experiment output must include:
- **Runner code** committed to `scripts/lab/` or `eval/` (no ephemeral notebook-only experiments)
- **Dataset hash** (SHA256 of the eval corpus DB or JSONL file)
- **Exact nox-mem version** (`git rev-parse HEAD` at time of run)
- **Environment** (Node version, sqlite-vec version, Gemini model version)

Without these four artifacts, the experiment cannot be replicated and is not considered complete.

### Publication

Every Lab item produces a published artifact regardless of outcome:
- **Positive result:** `audits/<date>-<name>.md` + potential paper PR
- **Negative result:** `audits/<date>-<name>-null.md` — describes what was tried, why it did not work, and what future approaches might succeed
- **Inconclusive:** `audits/<date>-<name>-inconclusive.md` — describes confounders and what would be needed to resolve

Null and inconclusive results are **explicitly valued**. The D47 decision (caminho C) applies to Lab equally.

### Honest Framing

If any benchmark shows nox-mem trailing a competitor: publish the gap, analyze why, and describe the targeted fix. Do not suppress results or narrow the evaluation scope post-hoc.

This is cross-linked to `docs/DECISIONS.md` D47 (caminho C reframe): the entire competitive narrative rests on being the system that publishes honest numbers.

### Shadow-Mode Discipline

Any Lab finding that proposes a ranking or scoring change must enter **shadow mode** for ≥7 days on production traffic before deployment. Hard rule — established via incident analysis and cravada in `[[ship-ranking-changes-in-shadow-mode-first]]`.
